### Improving Confidence, Grades, and Executive Functioning with Coding and Game Development
Turtle Graphics (also known as “Turtle Art”) is a classic educational computer program with roots going back to the 1960s. 

Co-developed alongside children by researchers at MIT, it teaches math and computer science in an intuitive, embodied, and surprisingly powerful way—accessible to kids as young as preschool age, but useful even in college-level math and physics classes.

Lua is a simple and accessible programming language best known for its role in the gaming sector, used in games like Balatro, Roblox, and World of Warcraft. 

Putting these two technologies together, students of any age can learn programming and math from the ground up with Lua and Turtle Graphics, improving their grades, executive functioning, and confidence with code art and game development.

Note: because of the concrete, step-by-step ways of thinking that Turtle Graphics encourages, these methods are especially useful for students who are sensitive, twice-exceptional, have been told they “don’t have a brain for math,” or could benefit from individualized and detailed attention. 

However, even kids with a go-getter attitude towards math, programming, or game development will find plenty to occupy their attention and new discoveries to embrace.
